System Architecture

Understanding how FruitLive's intelligent architecture creates value

The FruitLive Ecosystem

The Brain coordinates all farms and shared resources – ensuring optimal allocation, compliance tracking, and data-driven decision making across your entire portfolio.

Key Architectural Principles

Every piece of data exists in one place. When a worker's details are updated, it reflects instantly across all farms. When a vehicle is transferred, both the origin and destination farm see the change in real-time. No duplicate records, no confusion.

Modules talk to each other. Labour costs feed into crop profitability. Chemical applications link to compliance records. Water usage connects to block allocation. This integration eliminates data silos and manual re-entry.

Each user sees exactly what they need – nothing more, nothing less. Farm managers access only their farm's data. Field workers capture data through simplified mobile interfaces. Super admins control the entire portfolio.

Data becomes actionable intelligence instantly. See labour costs by block updated hourly. Monitor water usage against license limits in real-time. Track compliance scores as documents are uploaded. Make decisions based on current reality.

Real-World Use Cases

How FruitLive solves actual farming challenges

Challenge: Quinn Farms enters pruning season. Rich needs 160 workers but only has 147 permanent staff. Kinmount Estate's harvest is done – they have capacity.

FruitLive Solution:

  1. Tam logs into Admin → Shared Resources
  2. Sees Kinmount has 25 available workers
  3. Creates transfer: 12 workers, Kinmount → Quinn Farms, 09 Dec - 23 Dec
  4. Both Rich and Johannes get notifications
  5. Workers show up in Rich's daily allocation automatically
  6. Costs allocated to Quinn Farms, no manual invoicing needed

Result: Resource gap filled in 5 minutes. No phone calls, no spreadsheets, no billing confusion.

Challenge: Global GAP audit scheduled for 15 Jan 2026. Auditor will ask for spray records, training certificates, water usage logs, chemical storage certificates, and more.

FruitLive Solution:

  1. Compliance module shows 94% readiness score
  2. 3 training certificates flagged as expiring – renew immediately
  3. Export spray records for all blocks (includes: chemical, PHI, operator, weather)
  4. Water module shows full irrigation records, license compliance
  5. Chemical module links every application to disposal certificates
  6. Generate complete digital audit pack in 2 hours

Result: Auditor impressed. Zero findings. Certificate renewed with confidence.

Challenge: Middle East importer finds bruised apples in consignment CONS-2025-042. Needs full traceability within 24 hours or risks losing contract.

FruitLive Solution:

  1. Post-Harvest → Traceability tab
  2. Search consignment: CONS-2025-042
  3. System shows: Block A3, Rows 12-15, picked 05 Feb by Team 3 (Johannes - Induna)
  4. Last spray: 15 Jan (Captan 80 WP, PHI 14 days – compliant)
  5. Packed: 06 Feb, Line 2, Pallet PLT-2025-0284
  6. Cold chain: 0.5°C maintained throughout, no breaks
  7. Generate PDF report with complete chain in 10 minutes

Result: Issue traced to rough handling at pack house (unrelated to farm). Contract saved. Annual mock recall exercise proven invaluable.

Challenge: Tam wants to know: Are we spending too much on pruning low-value varieties? Which blocks should we expand vs. phase out?

FruitLive Solution:

  1. Labour module shows: Golden Delicious blocks consume 70% of pruning hours
  2. Post-Harvest module shows: Golden Delicious only generates 30% of revenue
  3. Crop Monitoring shows: Granny Smith has better sizing consistency year-on-year
  4. Chemical costs by block reveal: Pink Lady blocks require 40% more spray applications
  5. Generate profitability report per variety across all farms

Result: Strategic decision: Phase out underperforming Golden Delicious blocks, expand Granny Smith. Data-backed, not gut feel.
